The PSP

A Marvel

Features

In Japanese market PSPs, an application called x-Radar Portable came preloaded starting with firmware version 6.35 (January 2010). This is a port of a map software for PCs and mobile phones called x-Radar, developed by PetaMap. x-Radar Portable gets the location of the PSP on a map and obtains information of places around the user. It determines the location through "PlaceEngine" technology, via wireless LAN spots.

Specifications

The PSP is powered by an 1800 mAh battery (1200 mAh on the 2000 and 3000 models) that provides between four and six hours of gameplay, between four and five hours of video playback, or between eight and eleven hours of audio playback. The battery takes approximately 1.5 hours to charge and lasts approximately 500 charges. The PSP is equipped with a two-pin docking connector on the bottom of the console allowing users to connect the PSP to a television or a monitor using a component or composite cable. The PSP Slim features an AV output on the top of the console, allowing users to play games and watch videos on a television screen.

Price

The PSP was released in Japan on December 12, 2004, in North America on March 24, 2005, and in the PAL region on September 1, 2005. It primarily competed with the Nintendo DS, as part of the seventh generation of video game consoles. The PlayStation Portable was the most powerful portable console when launched. It was the first real competitor to Nintendo's handheld domination, where many challengers, such as SNK's Neo Geo Pocket and Nokia's N-Gage, failed.
